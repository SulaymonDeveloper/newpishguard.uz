Visual clues that reveal a phishing attempt

Learn to identify phishing attacks by recognizing visual red flags in usernames, logos, and design

Lesson progress 0%

üìñ Common visual clues

  • Slight misspellings in usernames (e.g., @Teleqram vs @Telegram).
  • Wrong logos, fake blue checkmarks, or poor design copies of official profiles.
  • Overuse of capital letters and exclamation marks to create urgency.

These visual details are often the first and easiest way to identify a phishing attempt. Cybercriminals rely on users not noticing small differences in usernames, colors, or tone.
