üìñ Understanding phishing links

Cybercriminals often use shortened links (like bit.ly) or redirecting domains (like t.me) to disguise malicious URLs. Some even use new or strange domain extensions such as .zip or .top to trick users.

On a desktop, hover your mouse over a link to preview its real destination. On mobile, long-press the link before opening it.
