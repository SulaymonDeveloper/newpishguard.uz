üìß Email phishing

This is the most common type of phishing. Attackers send fake emails pretending to be from banks, tech companies, or colleagues. The goal is to trick you into clicking a link or opening an attachment.

üí° Example

"Dear Customer, your bank account has been locked for security reasons. Click below to verify your details."

In smishing, attackers send text messages that appear to come from legitimate services like delivery companies or government agencies.

"Your package is waiting for delivery confirmation. Please confirm your address: http://mydelivery-update.info"

üìû Voice phishing (Vishing)

Vishing involves phone calls or voice messages that pressure you to share information or make payments. Attackers often impersonate customer support or IT departments.

üí° Example

"This is John from your IT department. We detected malware on your system. Please provide your login details so we can fix it."
